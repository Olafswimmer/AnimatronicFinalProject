Splash Mountain

Splash Mountain is a well-known and extremely popular flume ride at Magic Kingdom in Walt Disney World. It travels through the story of Brer Rabbit leaving his home and travelling to "the Laughin' Place" and then to the Briar Patch to find a new home, away from Brer Fox and Brere Bear, who try to hunt him down. The ride features many animatronic animals with fairly complex movements, including some figures that move across scenes as well as move their bodies. One such example is a animatronic of Brer Rabbit that is located in a scene where he is meant to run across a the cojntryside toward his now home, right after he leaves his old one. This animatronic begins moving after a female rabbit points at him and says "there goes Brer Rabbit." But, this animatronic has had a lot of issues and malfunctions, slowly leading to a time where there is an empty field and no Brer Rabbit.

Hopping Through a Scene

"my oh my what a wonderful day... for broken animatronics" The hopping version of Brer Rabbit was a revolutionary figure when it was created in 1992. Since his feet don't touch the ground, the programming must all be transmitted through his metal arm support, rather than him standing on the ground and having a machine that gives him cues right beneath him, which was a difficult change. The rabbit also had a large range of motion, and many programmed joints, including his knees, ankles, hips, and ear joints, and needed to be extremely light so that he coulf be held up in the right position. But, since this animatronic was a new idea, the figure was bound to experience issues. After becoming nutorious for just breaking with no explanation, the hopping Brer Rabbit became known as the "million dollar rabbit" because of the emmense cost of his revival each time he broke down. As time went on, after his introduction into the ride, he slowly began losing his large range of motion, "million dollar rabbit" and some of his joints appeared to begin locking up. His completely limp mode became increasingly common, until one day, when the entire animatronic had disappeared behind a brand new plastic shrub prob.

Dead Bunny

While it is admirable that maintnence gave the dead bunny animatronic some privacy with a plastic prop, it is disappointing to see the animatronic disappear. If guests turned around in their seats after they passed the scene, the rabbit could be easily seen, just laying on the ground in his ready position, but never beginning the arc across the scene. On the rare occasion that he was switched on, he was no longer realistic or impressive, since most of the time the scene consisted of a metal arm carrying a limp bunny across a countryside. Often, each of the joints that had a movement function programmed either went stiff, so the bunny looked possessed, or limp, so the bunny looked dead. Neither were something that any guest wanted to see, resulting in the switched off version of the bunny once again.

Retheming?

"the Zip-I-Dee-Doo-Dah day is coming to an end" Since Brer Rabbit has been lifeless for quite a long time now, with no mention of a fix in the future, the chances of a fix to the old, or even a replacement are becoming increasingly slim. The probability of a fix dwindled to near 0% when in July of 2020, Walt Disney Imagineering released a statement regrading the retheme of the entire attraction at Walt Disney World to a Princess in the Frog Bayou attraction. While there is no news regrarding when the retheme will occur, other than the fact that it will occur after Disney World's 50th anniversery in October 2021, it is still highly likely that Brer Rabbit's Zip-I-Dee-Do-Dah day will end in the next few years, making a fix of the broken Brer Rabbit irrelevant. While imagineering did make a temporary fix to the figure, having the metal arm move in the correct arc to simulate hopping, the animatronic was still not at 100% and quickly went back to a limp statue lying behind a shrub. This seemed like an attempt to fix him for good and obviously did not work well, but one can hope that before the ride closes for an extended closure to remove all the animal animatronics and become Princess and the Frog themed, Brer Rabbit will be returned to his former glory, hopping through the field with a full range of motion and a smile on his face.
